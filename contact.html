About

Student filmmaker focused on cinematography, emotional compression, and visual structure.

I am a student filmmaker drawn to the discipline of cinematography and to the challenge of compressing emotion into narrative, image, and rhythm. My work is shaped by a strong interest in short films that do not simply tell a story, but distill a feeling—something uneasy, tender, restless, or difficult to name—into a form that can be seen and felt at once. I am especially interested in the point where visual composition and emotional structure begin to speak the same language.

Much of what I make begins in ordinary scenes: light on a wall, a passing street, the quiet pressure of a familiar place, the subtle fracture within a routine moment. From these fragments, I build short films and visual pieces centered on mood, sensation, and the emotional texture of everyday life. I care deeply about framing, movement, color, and atmosphere, and I see cinematography not as ornament, but as a way of thinking—one that can carry meaning with precision and force.

I am still a student, still learning, and still refining my voice through each project. At the same time, I work with seriousness, curiosity, and a growing confidence in cinematography and color grading, both of which continue to develop rapidly through practice. I am open to collaboration, especially on projects that value visual intention, emotional depth, and a willingness to experiment. Feel free to contact me.
